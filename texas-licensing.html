TEXAS LICENSING GUIDE

How To Become A Licensed Life Insurance Agent In Texas

A simple roadmap for future Zachry Insurance agents who want to understand the Texas licensing process before getting started.

View The Steps
BEFORE YOU START

What This Guide Covers

This page is an educational overview, not legal or licensing advice. Always verify requirements directly with the Texas Department of Insurance before applying.

Exam First

Texas resident life agent applicants generally need to pass the Texas licensing exam before submitting the license application.

Fingerprints

Most applicants must complete a fingerprint background check and keep proof that fingerprints were sent properly.

Application

After passing the exam, applicants can apply online through Sircon or the National Insurance Producer Registry.

STEP BY STEP

Texas Licensing Roadmap

Use this as a basic checklist while you prepare for a life insurance career.

1

Choose The Right License Path

Many new life insurance agents look at a Life Agent license or a General Lines Life, Accident, Health and HMO license depending on the products they plan to sell.

2

Study For The Texas Exam

Take your preparation seriously. A prep course can help you learn insurance terms, state rules, policy basics, and exam-style questions.

3

Schedule And Pass The Exam

Texas insurance licensing exams are handled through Pearson VUE. Keep your exam documentation because your application must match your exam information.

4

Complete Fingerprints

Start the fingerprint process through TDI's online portal, then follow the instructions for IdentoGO electronic fingerprinting.

5

Apply Online

Submit the license application online through Sircon or NIPR. Make sure the application is complete before submitting.

6

Keep Learning After Licensing

Licensed agents must stay compliant with continuing education and renewal requirements. Build the habit of checking requirements early.
